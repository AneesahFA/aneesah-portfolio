Artifact 1: Air Indiana Chatbot

Title: Air Indiana Chatbot Project

Objective: To learn how to design, develop, and deploy a conversational AI chatbot tailored to airline customer service needs.

Description: This chatbot simulates a virtual assistant for Air Indiana, guiding users through flight inquiries, booking steps, and general support. The chatbot responds intelligently to user input using ChatGPT’s built-in conversational capabilities.

Live Demo: Try the Air Indiana Chatbot

Process: I used ChatGPT to create and train the chatbot by feeding it sample questions and evaluating its responses. I refined its training data by asking ChatGPT how it would respond to various user inputs, effectively teaching it how to handle common airline-related inquiries. The chatbot uses its own AI capabilities along with the contextual guidance I provided to deliver accurate and engaging responses.

Tools & Technologies: ChatGPT, prompt design, testing iterations, GitHub Pages for portfolio deployment

Value Proposition: Demonstrates my ability to build AI-driven user experiences, reflecting both technical and soft skills like communication and iteration.

Unique Value: This project shows how AI and user-driven data training can be combined to enhance digital customer service in the airline industry.

Artifact 2: Computer Vision Project

Title: Understanding the Scope and Impact of Computer Vision

Objective: To research, design, and present a comprehensive overview of computer vision technologies, key innovations, and applications.

Description: This presentation walks through the evolution of computer vision, from early AI to deep learning breakthroughs. It highlights challenges in real-time processing, data generalization, and quality, and offers insight into how this field is transforming modern industries. Computer Vision is one of the most practical and transformative applications of artificial intelligence today. It allows machines to interpret and make decisions based on visual input, such as images or video, just like humans do. For instance, tools like Google Lens use computer vision to analyze what your camera sees and return context aware results. You can identify plants, scan QR codes, translate text in real time, or find similar products online just by pointing your phone. Similarly, on devices like the Samsung Galaxy S23 Ultra, when you tap and hold an object in a photo, the phone uses advanced object segmentation and recognition to isolate the subject, then offers options to search the web or interact with it. These experiences are powered by a combination of deep learning, edge AI, and sophisticated image processing, all under the umbrella of computer vision. This project highlights how these capabilities are built and used across industries, from mobile apps to medical diagnostics and smart city systems.

Tools & Technologies: PowerPoint, research on AI models like CNNs, ViTs, SAM, and real-world case studies

Relevance: Aligned with learning objectives in artificial intelligence, machine learning, and ethical use of technology in society.

Artifact: Campus Recruitment System

Title: Campus Recruitment System

Objective: To design and implement a centralized, role-based web platform that automates the student recruitment process within a college campus.

Description: This project facilitates online recruitment by allowing students to apply for jobs, job providers to post openings, and administrators to manage the overall process. It aims to replace traditional manual systems with a secure and efficient digital solution.

Process: The system was developed using JSP, Servlets, and Oracle 10g. The workflow involved requirements gathering, modular design of admin, job provider, and student roles, followed by implementation and testing with real-world scenarios. Mockups were later created to visualize the system interfaces.

Tools and Technologies Used: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, JSP, Servlets, Oracle 10g, Apache Tomcat, MyEclipse, Canva, AI-powered mockup tools

Value Proposition: Demonstrates my ability to develop and manage full-stack systems with real-world relevance. It showcases backend logic, database handling, user authentication, and modular role management.

Unique Value: Specifically built for campus placement needs, this system connects students, recruiters, and faculty in one streamlined platform that supports transparency and efficiency.

Relevance: This project reflects my academic learning and practical application in computer science and highlights my readiness for backend and full-stack development roles.

Reflection

This project helped me understand the dynamics of multi-role systems, backend data operations, and the importance of a user-friendly interface. The experience taught me how to approach system design thoughtfully and how important structured communication between roles is in building scalable and maintainable platforms. I also learned the value of presenting my work clearly through mockups and documentation, especially for non-technical stakeholders.
